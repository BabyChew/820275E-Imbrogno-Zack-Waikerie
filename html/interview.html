Interview Questions

In the world of technology and gaming, Artificial Intelligence (AI) plays a vital role, both as a subject of fascination and as a practical tool. In this series of questions, we delve into the realm of AI and its applications within the exciting domain of video games. Whether you're a casual gamer, a developer, or simply curious about the intersection of AI and gaming, we aim to understand your experiences and insights. So, let's explore your familiarity with AI, your experiences in creating or interacting with AI in video games, and the level of effort and expertise required in this captivating field.

Question 1: How familiar are you with Artificial Intelligence?

This question aims to gauge the respondent's general knowledge and familiarity with the concept of Artificial Intelligence.

Follow-up: How familiar are you with Artificial Intelligence in video games?

This follow-up seeks to understand the respondent's specific knowledge and experience regarding the application of AI in the context of video games.

Question 2: What experience have you had with artificial intelligence?

This question asks about the respondent's overall experience working with or encountering AI in any context.

Follow-up: What experience have you had with artificial intelligence in video games?

Here, the focus is on the respondent's specific experiences related to AI in video games, such as playing games with AI opponents or developing AI for video games.

Question 3: How much work does it take to create an AI?

This question is more general and seeks to understand the effort and resources required to create AI, possibly in any context.

Follow-up: How much work does it take to create an AI in a video game?

This follow-up narrows down the scope to creating AI specifically for video games, aiming to get insights into the complexities and efforts involved.

Question 4: Have you ever created an AI for fun?

This question asks if the respondent has ever created AI, whether for recreational purposes or in a professional capacity. It also acknowledges the potential sensitivity of AI used in governmental contexts.

Follow-up: How long did it take you to create this AI, and how sophisticated was it?

If the respondent has created AI, this follow-up inquires about the duration of the project and the level of sophistication achieved in the AI system.

Question 5: What did this AI do that you created? (IF one was made)

If the respondent has created AI, this question asks for details on the AI's functionality and purpose.

Question 6: Have you ever played a game with an AI (If so, what was your favorite one)

This question asks about the respondent's experience playing games that feature AI opponents and their favorite game in this context.

Follow-up: What did the AI do, and how much work do you think went into it?

In this follow-up, the respondent is asked to provide insights into the AI's role in the game and to estimate the development efforts that went into creating it.

Question 7: What do you need to create an Artificial Intelligence in a video game?

This question explores the essential components or requirements for developing AI in the context of video games.

Question 8: Could someone with some IT experience create an AI in a video game?

This question queries whether individuals with a basic level of IT (Information Technology) knowledge can create AI systems for video games, assessing the accessibility of this field to beginners. These questions and follow-ups provide a comprehensive view of the respondent's AI knowledge, experience, and insights into AI in the context of video games.

Respondents Response

In this series of responses, Chris Tyler shares their insights and experiences related to artificial intelligence, with a particular focus on its application within the realm of video games. Chris, who has a background in AI and game development, provides valuable perspectives on AI familiarity, practical experience, and the complexities of creating AI in a gaming context. Let's delve into Chris Tyler's thoughts and experiences in the exciting intersection of AI and video games.

Question 1 and Follow-up:

Chris Tyler has a good understanding of AI, particularly because they studied it during their university education. They have encountered AI in video games and are familiar with various types of game AI, from entity AI to simulating players.

Question 2 and Follow-up:

Chris Tyler has practical experience working with AI in different projects, which is a valuable aspect of their expertise. They highlight a capture-the-flag scenario and a network of agents, demonstrating their ability to work on diverse AI applications.

Question 3 and Follow-up:

Chris Tyler explains the variability in the effort required to create AI, emphasizing that it depends on the complexity of the task. They provide a structured approach to AI development, highlighting the importance of goal setting, step-by-step development, and rigorous testing.

Question 4 and Follow-up:

Chris Tyler has experimented with AI in game development, specifically in a turn-based RPG game. They acknowledge the simplicity of their AI compared to more complex applications.

Question 5:

Chris Tyler's AI was designed to make in-game enemies behave in a lifelike manner, using a mix of attacks and conditional rules to achieve this.

Question 6 and Follow-up:

Chris Tyler mentions playing Age of Empires 2 and Baldur's Gate 3 as examples of games with sophisticated AI. They recognize the immense amount of work that goes into creating sophisticated AI for games, especially for real-time and complex strategy games.

Question 7:

Chris Tyler emphasizes the importance of AI knowledge, testing, and having a clear goal when creating AI in video games.

Question 8:

Chris Tyler believes that individuals with some IT experience can create AI in video games, highlighting that programming skills are key to this endeavor.

Summary:

Chris Tyler's responses showcase a strong understanding of AI concepts, a good mix of theoretical knowledge and practical experience, and a pragmatic approach to AI development in video games.
